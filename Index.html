About this project

BioLog is a personal project born from a simple problem: learning in biotechnology is scattered. Notes in notebooks, slides in folders, observations in margins — nothing connected.

This is an attempt to change that. A space where learning feels alive — where a cell image connects to a technique, which connects to a concept, which connects to a question not yet answered.

More than a study tool, BioLog is a record of curiosity. The questions matter as much as the answers. The confusion matters as much as the clarity.

Open Notebook

A sample observation entry — the format every cell image will follow.

🔬 Cell / Sample
Onion Epidermal Cell — Allium cepa
🧫 Preparation
Wet mount, stained with iodine solution (Lugol's)
🔍 Magnification
40× objective · 400× total magnification
🧬 Structures Observed
Cell wall (rigid, rectangular), central vacuole, nucleus (stained amber by iodine), no chloroplasts visible — consistent with non-photosynthetic tissue.
📖 Scientific Explanation
The onion epidermal cell is a classic model for plant cell observation. The cell wall provides structural rigidity, while the large central vacuole — filled with cell sap — pushes the cytoplasm and nucleus against the cell membrane. Iodine reacts with starch, but since the vacuole of epidermal cells contains little starch, the staining primarily highlights the nucleus.
💭 Personal Reflection
What struck me was how clearly the cell boundaries were defined. Each cell like a room in a building — its own contained space, but part of something larger. I spent ten minutes just moving across the slide, watching the regularity of it all.
